Catherine Brys PhD(Eng) MBA



Catherine has deep expertise in Customer Experience Excellence and loves helping companies improve their client experiences. She is experienced at coaching leaders to develop an ambitious vision for the future. Catherine’s clients appreciate her strategic thinking and how she keeps them focused on their vision for success. She understands that for change to be successful it needs to be co-created with staff and stakeholders, and she is an experienced facilitator. Catherine’s extensive experience as a Change Manager in the private and public sector and in academia means that she has a treasure trove of insights and practical experience she can tap into.

Catherine has an MBA (with specialism Strategy) from Strathclyde Business School. She has a degree and PhD in Engineering/Physics from the University of Ghent, Belgium. She is a trained workplace coach and also a negotiation and conflict resolution professional (Postgraduate Certificate in Mediation, Conflict Resolution and Negotiation from Strathclyde University). She is in particular fascinated by inter-personal and group dynamics. In her spare time she volunteers as a Lead mediator at Strathclyde University Mediation Clinic.

Catherine excels at helping people see clarity in complex situations and formulate an ambitious vision for the future. Her scientific mindset allows her to systematically but quickly diagnose issues and unearth root causes. Catherine is able to guide clients to develop a pragmatic action plan that will help them realise their vision for success.

“I’ve worked with Catherine for over 5 years. Catherine combines a strategic mindset with a highly structured and analytical approach. This makes her a very valuable asset in developing as well as reviewing strategies and new ideas. She’s acted as a great sounding board for some of my own thinking and can be counted on to offer new and valuable insights.”
Ewan Mearns, Senior Manager, Strategy & Sectors at Scottish Enterprise

When working with clients, Catherine focuses on short interventions that enthuse and create momentum, and result in pragmatic actions that clients can execute.

“Catherine recently facilitated our Board strategy day. We had been looking at how we could integrate a conversation around our purpose and values aligning with equality and diversity objective and at the same time identifying key issues for the next three years. Catherine delivered a balanced workshop that met all of those objectives. The overall experience worked well and everyone has mentioned the need to ‘keep the momentum’ developed during the strategy day as we refine objectives arising from the day. What Catherine achieved was develop that momentum in an online event which is not an easiest thing to do. “
